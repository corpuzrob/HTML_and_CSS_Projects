Why Do We Have Daylight Saving Time?

Twice a year, the vast majority of us change our clocks, either springing forward or falling back. But why? Check out the video below for a history lesson in daylight saving time, and an argument for how and why to fix it:

Okay, so there are definitely drawbacks to our current system, including increased risk of health issues and accidents due to disrupting our sleep schedules and lost business revenue due to mistakes on the job.

Plus it just generally sucks to lose an hour of sleep in the spring and to see the sun go down an hour earlier in the fall.

So what are our alternatives? One, we could just stay permanently on daylight saving time.

Two, we could go back to having standard time year-round.

Let's explore both possibilities:


Solutions:

Argument for Permanent Daylight Savings Time:

  1. We'll spend more time outside.
  2. Higher sales for many businesses.
  3. Less crime.
  4. Sun won't rise super early on summer mornings

Argument for Permanent Standard Time:

  1. Easier on farmers.
  2. May sync better with our circadian biology.
  3. It sucks to go to work and school in the dark.
